My Favorite Color: White

Haha, you actually thought I was going to talk about my favorite color. Fact of the matter is, no professional ever talks about their favorite color. Luckily, a professional is something that I am not. The favorite color thing is way too cliche, but I'll provide you with one sentence about it anyway because there's no point in just leaving this section here to rot.

My favorite color is white not because it's supposed to symbolize purity but because if I had to be stuck in a room I'd want the walls to be white and not #6a006a. Let's move along, shall we?

My Favorite Number: 8

Eight is my absolutely favorite number, in honor of one of my Favorite (lol) fictional characters: Vriska Serket. Her entire character is based around the number eight, even the way she types. But that is not why I like her! I like her because she's a very dynamic character, and people can relate to her at many times. You know, when I was little I used to claim that thirteen was my favorite number mostly because people considered it unlucky. Needless to say, I felt very proud of myself for making a number others didn't like my favorite. I then learned that literally every other child aiming to be rebellious decided that thirteen was their favorite number as well and I suddenly did not feel very cool.

Another reason eight is my favorite number is because of this other fictional character (I'll admit that I actually don't like him as much anymore), known as Death the Kid. However, most people just call him that one really OCD guy who's all about things being symmetrical. His favorite number is also eight, in case you haven't figured that out. Though I'm not really into Soul Eater, when I first saw him, I thought, 'This guy is super cool. Whatever he likes, I like (probably).' It's really, really dumb in retrospect, but I was young and foolish back then or something.

In conclusion, my favorite number was inspired by these two super characters.

My Favorite Crossover Fighting Game: Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3

Ultimate Marvel vs. Capcom 3, or UMVC3, was released on the fifteenth of November in 2011. Now that I think about it, this game is pretty old. Anyways, it has an inferior version called Marvel vs. Capcom 3: Fate of Two Worlds. The difference between the two is minimal, but it's enough to make me like UMVC3 more than its poor little brother. Capcom and Marvel tried pulling a fast one on us by releasing a 'better' version of MVC3, and it sadly worked. In fact, I think I can actually reenact what their conversation probably sounded like when they were thinking of creating UMVC3:

CAPCOM: Yeah, so, uh, we kinda need to make more money off of our players. DLC just isn't cutting it these days.

MARVEL: Oh, how about we, uh...I know! We'll just create an enhanced version of MVC3.

CAPCOM: But how are we going to make the game different from the last one?

MARVEL: Uhmmm...we'll just add a couple of new modes and characters and pass it off as original! The players are so stupid, they'll probably notice but shove their money at us anyway.

CAPCOM: Brilliant! Let's get working on it right away.

Now, if I was a random person reading this, I would probably be thinking, 'Does she hate this game or something? She's only listing out its flaws and how the two collaborating companies tried to rip her off!' While all of that is true, you can tell that the creators worked hard on the game (even if they are cheap as all heck). The art, animation, music, and all of the other goodies that come with it make it worth playing for at least two hours. There's online battling, a training mode, a two player mode, endings for all of the players in arcade mode, a gallery, and so much more.

My Favorite Shark: Whale Shark

I wanted to describe to you eight of my favorite things, but UMVC3 took up way too much room and I don't have that many favorites anyway. For these reasons, I will end this lovely Favorites page with my favoritest (this is an actual word) creature in the whole, wide Earth: the whale shark!

Take a few moments to observe this majestic creature.

I used to think they were orange for some odd, unexplainable reason. Further research on my part has revealed that they are more #252D3A than orange, and their bellies are a pure #FFFFFF. You can blame Go, Diego, Go! for making me think they were orange. I think that show first introduced me to whale sharks...?

Whale sharks are very docile and live deep where humans cannot kill them off for the heck of it. If I had a pet whale shark, I would in all likelihood kill it by accident. Taking care of something that huge and awesome just wouldn't work. Fish and plankton do not think of whale sharks as their BFFs either because they are just plain jealous they will never be able to be as cool or because whale sharks can inhale them as quickly as a kindergartner can inhale Pixy Stix on Halloween.

Whale sharks are so docile, in fact, that divers sometimes just hitch a ride on their backs and chill there for a while. I suspect that the reason the whale shark doesn't mind is because it has come to terms with the realization that they are the most amazing creatures on Earth, and therefore can't get angry when random meaty stick figures decide that they want to hang around on their backs for a couple of minutes. If you take the time to search my theory up, you will find that what I am saying is absolutely true and everything you are reading right now is changing your life.
