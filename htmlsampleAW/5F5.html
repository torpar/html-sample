Games:

You know the drill by now. Click 'Games.'

Have you ever wanted to play some online games but you just can't because...well, actually, I don't know. But have you ever just wanted to play a game and for some odd, unexplainable reason you can't? WELL HERE ARE SOME AWESOME SITES AND GAMES, BRO:

Miniclip, a site with a large variety of mini games.

Nitrome has tons of cute pixel-style mini games.

The Majesty of Colors, a short but interesting game about a sea monster and the world around it.

Tiny Dangerous Dungeons, an awesome 8-bit dungeon platformer with rad music.

Haunt the House, a Halloweenesque game about a ghost trying to get its home back from partying people.

Enough of the online games for now. Next, I'll present some virus-free PC games (Mac works for some of them too, though) that are off the charts in awesomeness: (Is 'awesomeness' a word?)

Irisu Syndrome, a somewhat creepy puzzle game. Here is the English patch. (Move the contents of the English patch into the 'data' folder.)

Ib, a horror game where a girl visits what first seems to be an ornate art gallery.

Misao, another horror game in which you must find a girl's...body parts. Great story, but extremely violent and not for the sensitive souls.

Wadanohara and the Great Blue Sea, a beautifully made 'pseudo-RPG' about a young witch who goes back to her sea after finding out that troubles have recently been plaguing it.

Alice Mare, a horroresque adventure game with a fairy tale theme.

LiEat ( 1,2,3 ), a short and cute RPG about a lie-eating dragon named Efina and her 'papa,' Teobaldo Leonhearts.

Yume Nikki, a horror RPG that involves exploring a girl named Madotsuki's dreams and collecting 'effects,' little eggs that give you different powers.

Websites:

Now click 'Websites.'

I shall now present you with some great websites that--while they don't involve games--are worth checking out because they're just amazing and that's all there really is to say on the matter.

deviantArt, a site with both inspiring and kind of stupid art. Mostly inspiring, but there are always those pictures that make me think, '???'

Pixiv, another site with lots and lots of beautiful art.

Tumblr, a social media platform that has way too many 'keyboard warriors' but is pretty cool in other regards.

Crunchyroll, an anime hosting site. It has lots of ads, which is pretty darn annoying. (Using Adblock seems to solve the problem, though.)

Amazon. Just...Amazon.

Pandora, a music hosting site that charges you music for the low, low price of free and some ads unless you use Adblock.

Mogeko, one of my favorite artists ever. Including their site was just very necessary.

Codecademy and W3schools, a great pair of sites where you can learn about programming for the low, low price of free. That's my kind of price.
